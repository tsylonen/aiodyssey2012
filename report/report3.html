AI Odyssey 2012

Challenge 3: Space War

In the last challenge of our course we were supposed to program an AI for the game Space War, a real time strategy game in which two players struggle for the ownership of a cluster of planets.

It would have been interesting to solve the problem with an algorithm derived with an evolutionary metaheuristic but I couldn't come up with a way to do that in a sensible way fast enough so I gave up on the idea.

I thought it would be a good idea to use the sample java bot as a base for my AI and started hacking away. I implemented a bot that would give planets scores were supposed to reflect the profitability of attacking them. The bot would then attack the most profitable one. It would try to figure out if the enemy could be killed right away by attacking furiously and attempt to do so.

I tried adding features to the bot but couldn't get them to work. I soon realised that the bot was full of bugs and I wasn't very eager to try to debug them so I did what any sensible programmer would do and began a rewrite from scratch in python on the last day of the challenge :-)

Even though I reversed my inequality signs and sorted lists in the wrong order uncountably many times it didn't take long to have my python bot beat my old java one.

I ended up with an algorithm that calculates a rough profitability index for each planet. Bigger planets are given a higher index than smaller ones and enemy planets are given a higher one than neutral ones. Also, the less ships there are present the better. Before these indexes are taken into account, my bot sends ships to planets with very small populations (less than 3) because otherwise it would often not attack them. Later versions of my bot would also look for enemy ships heading towards own planets and attempt to protect them from being overrun.

For some reason I wasn't able to run my newest versions of the bot on the server, the replays show them doing nothing even though they worked on my computer so I wasn't able to test any version that could protect its planets on the game server. Still, my bot seemed to work reasonably well against the organizers' bots but it did get beaten up quite badly by bots from other players.
